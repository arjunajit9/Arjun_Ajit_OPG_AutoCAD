# Privacy and Safety

Phase 1 accepts only images users confirm contain no direct identifiers. It does not collect names, contact details, dates of birth, addresses, or hospital numbers. Anonymous references can still be sensitive. The prototype cannot detect identifiers burned into image pixels.

Images and reviews are temporary browser data, expire after the configured period, and have an explicit deletion action. Browser storage is not approved clinical storage and does not guarantee secure deletion from backups or managed devices.

Before real data: require secure login, role-based authorization, TLS, encrypted object storage, metadata stripping, audit logging, session expiry, consent/privacy controls, retention and verified deletion policies, secrets management, incident response, access monitoring, and jurisdiction-specific legal/regulatory assessment.

The UI never claims diagnosis, normality, accuracy, or treatment advice. It begins with both teeth unmeasured, calculates only from examiner-positioned axes, and requires examiner verification. Unusable inputs must return `unable_to_analyse`, never invented findings.

For the thesis workflow, radiographic angulation is an exposure/risk indicator. Pericoronitis is not confirmed or excluded from the panoramic image; the study must use a prospectively defined clinical case definition and qualified examiner assessment.
