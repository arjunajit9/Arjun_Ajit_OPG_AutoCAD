# Dataset Requirements

Future development requires lawfully obtained, properly de-identified OPG images with documented provenance and representative acquisition conditions. Labels need precise clinical definitions, expert annotation guidelines, tooth-level and region-level annotations where appropriate, and versioned tooth-numbering rules.

Use multiple qualified specialist reviewers, measure agreement, and resolve disagreement through a predefined adjudication process. Keep patient-separated training, validation, internal test, and external test datasets. Avoid leakage across repeated examinations.

Evaluate across machines, sites, image qualities, acquisition protocols, demographics, and relevant patient groups. Report sensitivity, specificity, precision, recall, operating points, confidence intervals, calibration, subgroup performance, and failure-to-analyse rates. Give particular attention to false negatives and clinically consequential errors.

Dataset access, retention, consent, governance, security, bias review, clinical validation, and applicable regulatory review must be completed before real-world diagnostic use.
